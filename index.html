The Pain: Land Fraud is a Silent Killer

Nigeria's land registry system is plagued by inefficiencies and fraud that cost billions and hinder economic development.

Widespread Fraud

68% of land titles in Lagos and Abuja are fraudulent according to PwC 2023 report, creating massive distrust in the system.

Impact

Thousands of families lose their properties annually to fraudulent claims and documentation.

Massive Financial Loss

Nigerians lose ₦2.3 billion yearly to common land scams, with diaspora communities being particularly vulnerable.

Impact

Diaspora investments in Nigerian real estate are significantly hampered by trust issues.

Economic Paralysis

₦9.6 trillion in dead capital remains locked due to unreliable land titles, preventing economic development.

Impact

This dead capital could fund infrastructure, housing, and business development across Nigeria.

Why It Scales

Technology We Have Today

  • Smart Contracts: Deployed on Ethereum with testnet (Ropsten) for public verification
  • 500 Titles Digitized: SHA-3 hashes stored on-chain, documents on IPFS
  • Fraud Prevention: EFCC-validated detection of forged certificates and survey plan tampering
  • Hybrid Architecture: Combines public blockchain transparency with private blockchain efficiency
  • Mobile Integration: USSD and mobile app support for widespread accessibility

Near Term Roadmap (6 Months)

  • USSD Integration: MTN API access pending $15k budget
  • Hyperledger Migration: For government nodes (prototype ready)
  • QR Code Verification: In development for easy title verification
  • Diaspora Portal: Tokenized land sales platform for international investors
  • API Expansion: Developer tools for third-party integrations

Why This Scales

  • Low Operational Cost: ₦500/title vs ₦8,300 manual process
  • Offline Capability: Works without internet in rural areas
  • Efficient Workflow: Land Bureau → PDF scan → IPFS → Blockchain Hash → Owner verification
  • Government Partnership: Pilot program ready with FCT Land Bureau
  • Market Readiness: 84% of Abuja landowners willing to pay for verification services
